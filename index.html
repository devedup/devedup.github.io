Vubiquity White Label

Vubiquity is a "multiplatform video services, and linear content delivery" heavy weight, looking to move into mobile, we built a modular solution that could be easily integrated into third party apps, or quickly branded for new clients.

The solution consisted of a set of interchangeable modules (allowing for clients to plug in or replace existing modules), a customisable app, utilising the Microsoft PlayReady DRM video player and built using test driven development.

An internal content management system allowed for quickly customised client-branded apps for presentations.

Invesco

We created an iPhone and iPad app that enables you to watch training videos and then answer a series of multiple choice questions, after which you register your details and then submit to the server which presents you a certificate.

The initial version of the app was developed with meta data content packaged with it, then a second version was built which delivered content via a CMS.

The videos are downloaded or streamed on demand, and the app managed the download progress, pausing, deleting and continuation of the downloaded videos.

There are various logical navigation rules in the app, data storage and transmission, state management, animations, downloadable content.

Technology: Swift 1.1 on iOS 7 & 8

Galleryr Pro

Galleryr Pro is an iPad front end that I wrote to connect to Flickr which serves 6 billion+ photos and videos. I've been a Flickr user from the beginning and always fancied writing something to connect to their API. So here it is.... Well actually, this is my second vastly improved Flickr app, the first is called 'Flickr Gallery'. 

I connect to Flickr API via my own open source project on github FlickrKit, which was created especially for this project.

Airside

IFP

At APEX Expo 2013, IFP unveiled Airside, an "innovative new app that allows passengers to start watching a movie while they are in the lounge or at the gate, then continue watching the movie in-flight and even finish watching it at the hotel". With iBeacon technology to unlock new content in the airport lounge.

EE Film

EE Film was a customisation of the white label film app product that we developed. Everything Everywhere (now EE), were rebranding from T-Mobile/Orange, and wanted to incorporate cinema and Orange Wednesday 2 for 1 promotion. Alongside this, they wanted to showcase how fast their 4G network actually was. To celebrate the launch EE covered the mobile data costs of watching the HD movies on the go.

Extensive work was undertake on our white label product to product a bespoke solution for EE on the iPhone and iPad. It featured a secure Irdeto ActiveCloak DRM video player, basket/wishlist, voucher redemption and cinema search. We integrated with various third party APIs for cinema times and trailers. Customers could resume watching partially watched films across iOS devices, Android, web and set-top boxes.
